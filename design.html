Strategic Vision

Design
Strategy

The design strategy is a set of commitments — to the city, the river, the climate, and the people who will inhabit this place.

Five strategic commitments guide every decision: porosity (the building is more public than private at ground level), legibility (the structure is visible and comprehensible), adaptability (every floor plate can change use without structural modification), materiality (every material is chosen for its relationship with place), and longevity (designed for 200 years, not 30).

These commitments are tested at every design review. When a decision cannot be justified against at least three of the five, it is revised.

Design Matrix

"Good architecture is not a style — it is a discipline of sustained, principled decision-making."

Porosity

60% of the ground floor perimeter is publicly accessible — markets, galleries, cafés, and through-routes to the river.

Adaptability

6m structural grid allows all floors above the podium to convert between residential, office, and hotel use.

Longevity

Sacrificial external cladding, replaceable facade panels, and a 200-year structural lifespan in the brief.
